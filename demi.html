How to progress.

§ IIIa · WEEKLY PROGRESSION
  1. i

    Add reps. When 3×10 feels easy, progress to 3×12, then 3×15.

  2. ii

    Upgrade band tension. Light → medium → heavy.

  3. iii

    Change leverage. Incline push-up → flat → decline. Assisted single-leg → unassisted.

  4. iv

    Add tempo. Three-second descent, one-second pause, one-second up.

  5. v

    Add a set. Three sets → four sets.

Every working set logged. When all sets at top of rep range with full tempo feel easy, progress on the next session. Never two levers at once.

The rules.

§ IV · ADJUSTMENT TRIGGERS

If weight unchanged for four weeks and measurements unchanged

Drop 100 cal/day OR add a 20-min daily walk. Recomp stall, not failure.

If weight dropping more than 0.7 lb/week

Add 100 cal/day. The deficit is too aggressive — it will cost muscle.

If energy crashes, mood drops, sleep worsens

Eat at maintenance for one week. Reassess. Recovery is part of the program.

If lifts stop progressing for two weeks

Check sleep and protein first. If both are solid, deload one week.

If lower back flares

Skip the lower-body day. Substitute upper body or core + mobility. The injury comes first.

NON-NEGOTIABLE

If the menstrual cycle changes (irregular, missing, or notably different)

Stop the deficit immediately. Eat at maintenance. See a doctor if it persists. Female metabolism is sensitive to caloric stress in ways that overrule any aesthetic goal — this rule is non-negotiable.

Finis

The scale will not move. The mirror will. The DEXA will. The dumbbells will. Trust the slow build — the body is changing in places the scale cannot see.
