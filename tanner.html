Why zone-2

Slow on purpose.

Zone 2 is the aerobic floor — hard enough to train mitochondrial density and cardiac stroke volume, soft enough not to compete with strength recovery. For Tanner, the case is specific: visceral fat (1.91 lb) and an A/G ratio of 1.33 are the metabolic flags on the baseline scan, and zone-2 cardio is the single most evidence-backed intervention for both. Two sessions a week, 30–40 minutes each, at a heart rate where conversation stays possible. Not optional.

Mode Duration Notes

Schedule
Wednesday and Saturday. Wednesday breaks up the Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri block. Saturday is the long-and-easy day — pick the modality that fits weather and mood.

§ IV · ADJUSTMENT TRIGGERS

If gaining more than 0.7 lb/week

Drop calories by 200/day. The surplus is too aggressive — excess becomes fat, not muscle.

If gaining less than 0.3 lb/week

Add calories by 200/day. There isn't enough fuel reaching the tissue to grow.

If body fat crosses 32%

Pause the bulk. Run a four-to-six week mini-cut. Lose ten pounds of fat. Then resume building from a leaner base.

If lifts stall for two weeks

Deload. Drop weight 10%. Build back over two weeks. Check sleep and calories before blaming the program.

During the cut, if weight stalls two weeks

Take a one-week diet break at maintenance. Restore leptin. Then resume the deficit with fresh momentum.

Above all, resist weekly optimization

Twelve weeks before changing the plan. The body operates on a longer clock than the mind.

Finis

The hardest part won't be the workouts or the food. It will be the patience — accepting that the bulk feels slow and the cut feels long.
